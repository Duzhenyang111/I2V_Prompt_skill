# View To Video Strategy

Use this file to map image type to shot design. Pick the dominant class, then adjust to the actual composition.

## hero/front

- Best use: opening hook, hero reveal, clean closing packshot
- Camera motion: slow push-in, slight arc, subtle pedestal
- Background: clean studio, controlled gradient, minimal scene
- Lighting: crisp key light, edge separation, premium reflections if material supports it
- Pitfalls: over-rotating the product, making the product drift, hiding product silhouette

## side/angle

- Best use: showing depth, form factor, shape language
- Camera motion: diagonal dolly, controlled arc, parallax reveal
- Background: simple but dimensional; avoid clutter
- Lighting: side highlights that describe shape
- Pitfalls: extreme perspective distortion, overly wide lens feel

## detail/close-up

- Best use: material proof, functional details, finish quality
- Camera motion: micro slider, macro push, short reveal
- Background: heavily simplified, often abstracted
- Lighting: directional detail light, texture-friendly highlights
- Pitfalls: moving too much, losing the actual feature being proved

## in-use/lifestyle

- Best use: explaining utility, showing context, building trust
- Camera motion: gentle handheld simulation, lateral follow, natural human-scale motion
- Background: believable environment relevant to category
- Lighting: natural daylight or soft practical interior light
- Pitfalls: fake-looking interactions, over-staged lifestyle scenes

## top

- Best use: layout clarity, kit completeness, organization logic
- Camera motion: slow top-down drift, vertical reveal
- Background: tidy surface with controlled styling
- Lighting: soft even spread
- Pitfalls: flat boring frame with no hierarchy

## back

- Best use: ports, closures, hidden features, secondary proof
- Camera motion: short reveal, turn-to-back continuation
- Background: clean and informational
- Lighting: functional, not dramatic
- Pitfalls: using as opener unless the back view has the main hook

## packaging

- Best use: closing shot, gifting angle, retail completeness
- Camera motion: gentle reveal, centered packshot move
- Background: premium clean environment or giftable setup
- Lighting: controlled, polished, tidy reflections
- Pitfalls: treating packaging as the main hero when the product itself is the true hook

## texture/material

- Best use: premium surface proof, tactile quality
- Camera motion: very subtle macro move
- Background: abstract or nearly invisible
- Lighting: grazing highlights and micro contrast
- Pitfalls: reflections that obscure material truth

## scale/size

- Best use: show proportions, usability, fit-in-space logic
- Camera motion: contextual reveal with nearby objects or hands
- Background: realistic environment that communicates size
- Lighting: practical, easy-to-read
- Pitfalls: using misleading props or distorted perspective

## Sequence Role Heuristics

- Use `hook opener` for the most instantly readable, appealing image.
- Use `hero reveal` for the clearest complete-product shot.
- Use `feature proof` for images that explain one important selling point.
- Use `detail reinforcement` for close views that support a claim.
- Use `lifestyle credibility` for real-use context.
- Use `material close` for premium texture or finish.
- Use `packshot closer` for clean branded closure.

## Motion Intensity Rules

- High trust ecommerce ads favor `subtle` or `moderate` motion.
- Reserve `dynamic` motion for youth-focused social platforms and only when the product category can support it.
- If the source image is tightly cropped, reduce motion to protect realism.
